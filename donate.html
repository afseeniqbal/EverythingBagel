Knowing that you want to donate but not knowing where or how to can be hard. Don't worry, we are here to help you with that part! Below are a list of organizations to help you find local food pantries as well as America-wide organizations. Good Luck!

~Feeding America- Find Your Local Foodbank~
Feeding America assists millions of people annually by the statewide network of food banks and programs run by Feeding America in finding food and shopping assistance in their neighborhoods. To use this tool, type in your zip code or state information and the nearby food pantries will show up on the map provided.
~Ample Harvest- Find A Pantry~
Ample Harvest is a great directory tool for specific locations and addresses, and you can donate food as well as money through their organization. Directly through this site, you can also find places to access this food for if you are in need of assistance.
~Minnie's Food Pantry~
A nonprofit organization with its main office in Plano, Texas, Minnie's Food Pantry is dedicated to the idea that good nutrition and financial literacy are the foundations of general wellness. Through their organization, you may assist families in achieving long-term food security and end the generational curse of low-income residents (particularly children) lacking access to fresh produce.
~Second Harvest Heartland~
Second Harvest Heartland, the largest food bank in the area and a pioneer in the emergency food supply chain, depends on surplus food donations from business growers, merchants, and industry partners. Through Second Harvest Heartland, you may donate extra food to the biggest food bank in the area from your farm, store, factory, or commercial kitchen.
~Food Rescue Locator~
Food Rescue Locator is a resource that helps people and groups identify food recovery organizations so they may contribute extra food or provide other types of support. It is a database of organizations from all throughout the United States. You can enter into their system your address, and they will direct you to organizations close-by for you to donate your extra goods.
~Food Recovery Network~
In order to fight hunger and promote food justice, the Food Recovery Network organizes thousands of student leaders on college campuses throughout the country. Utilize their given map to locate the location that is closest to you, then donate your food there.
~Rock and Wrap It Up!~
You may donate your extra food to Rock and Wrap it Up and take part in their award-winning anti-poverty and anti-hunger think tank. There are also other side activities and projects you can participate in to fight food waste.
